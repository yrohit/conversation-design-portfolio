Design Process
01
Understand the task + stakes
Who is the user? What are the consequences of failure? What is the AI's authority boundary? These questions precede any dialogue design.
02
Design channel-native flows
Chat and voice are not adaptations of each other. Every flow is designed from scratch for its channel's cognitive constraints, timing model, and affordances.
03
Design failure before the happy path
Error states, ambiguous input, system failures, and user hesitation are designed first — not handled later. Every node has a defined recovery path.
04
Extract, document, and ship reusable patterns
Insights from one flow become patterns other teams can adopt. Documented with trigger conditions, failure modes, and decision logic — not just dialogue examples.
